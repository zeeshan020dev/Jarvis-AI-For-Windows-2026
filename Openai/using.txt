In the meantime, here's a general overview of common ways AI is used:

*   **Automation:** Automating repetitive tasks, from robotic process automation (RPA) in offices to industrial robots in factories.
*   **Data Analysis & Insights:** Processing vast amounts of data to identify patterns, make predictions, and inform decision-making (e.g., market trends, scientific research, fraud detection).
*   **Natural Language Processing (NLP):** Understanding and generating human language. This includes:
    *   **Chatbots and Virtual Assistants:** Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant, customer service bots.
    *   **Translation:** Google Translate.
    *   **Text Summarization & Generation:** Creating articles, reports, creative writing.
    *   **Sentiment Analysis:** Understanding the emotional tone of text.
*   **Computer Vision:** Enabling computers to "see" and interpret images and videos. This powers:
    *   **Facial Recognition:** Unlocking phones, security systems.
    *   **Object Detection:** Self-driving cars identifying pedestrians, traffic signs.
    *   **Medical Imaging Analysis:** Helping doctors detect diseases.
    *   **Quality Control:** Identifying defects in manufacturing.
*   **Recommendation Systems:** Personalizing content based on user preferences and past behavior (e.g., Netflix, Amazon, Spotify).
*   **Predictive Modeling:** Forecasting future events, such as weather, stock prices, customer churn, or equipment failures.
*   **Personalization:** Tailoring experiences, ads, and content to individual users.
*   **Healthcare:** Drug discovery, personalized medicine, diagnostic assistance, robotic surgery.
*   **Finance:** Algorithmic trading, fraud detection, credit scoring, personalized financial advice.
*   **Gaming:** Creating more intelligent NPCs (non-player characters) and dynamic game environments.
